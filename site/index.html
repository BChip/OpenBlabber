Open Blabber

A free, open-source English dictation keyboard for iPhone and iPad. Speak anywhere you can type — transcribed live, entirely on your device.

Private by design Your voice, transcripts, and typed text never leave your device. The model is bundled, with no server or runtime model download.
English, on purpose A compact streaming model focused on fast, efficient English dictation. No language picker, subscription, or bloat.
Open source A focused Swift codebase separates the companion app, lightweight keyboard, and protected local protocol. Licensed under the GPLv3.

How it works

  1. Install Open Blabber and enable the keyboard in Settings → General → Keyboard → Keyboards, including Allow Full Access.
  2. In any app, switch to the Open Blabber keyboard and tap its center button. Because iOS doesn't let keyboards use the microphone, the first tap opens Open Blabber. The local model prepares first and the microphone then starts automatically; use iOS's Back control to return.
  3. Tap the center mic, talk, and tap the same button to stop. Live recognized text and a voice-activity animation appear while you speak. Each dictation is capped at two minutes. Leaving or switching the keyboard releases the microphone and unloads the model automatically.

Live transcription is powered by the bundled, open-source Moonshine Tiny Streaming English model. Its roughly 49 MB of quantized assets run entirely in the companion app; the keyboard stays lean and receives only bounded text and an activity level.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 21, 2026

Open Blabber is built so your voice, transcripts, and typed content never leave your device. This policy explains the small amount of temporary, device-local data needed to pass a result from the app to its keyboard. The English speech model is included in the app, so transcription requires no server or runtime model download.

What we collect

No voice, transcript, or typed content leaves your device. Open Blabber has no service backend, user accounts, analytics, crash reporting, or advertising. We do not receive, sell, or share that content. The bundled model means the transcription path makes no network request. If you choose an external website or source-code link, iOS opens that destination outside the transcription path and its own privacy policy applies.

Your voice and your text

  • English transcription happens entirely on your device using the bundled Moonshine Tiny Streaming model, run locally by the open-source Moonshine Voice library. Your audio and transcribed text are never sent to us or to any third party by Open Blabber.
  • When Open Blabber opens, it prepares the local model and then starts the microphone automatically. The microphone remains available during the brief return-to-keyboard handoff and while the Open Blabber keyboard is present; leaving or switching keyboards shuts it down and unloads the model. A short heartbeat watchdog handles cases where iOS terminates the extension without a lifecycle callback.
  • Audio begins flowing to Moonshine only after you tap the keyboard's center mic, is processed as short memory-only chunks, is capped at two minutes per recording, and is never written to disk or logged. Open Blabber does not retain a second recording buffer; the keyboard receives text and a normalized activity level, never audio samples.
  • A finished transcript is placed in a temporary file in the app and keyboard's protected shared container so the keyboard can insert it. The keyboard deletes the file immediately after insertion or discard. An unconsumed result becomes invalid after two minutes and is scrubbed on the next app or keyboard mailbox check. Random request identifiers and short-lived engine state are stored alongside it only to prevent delivery to the wrong field.
  • For safe insertion, the keyboard briefly fingerprints a small amount of immediately surrounding cursor and selection context, and checks the preceding character to add natural spacing. It never writes or retains that context.

Permissions we ask for, and why

  • Microphone — required so the companion app can hear you while you dictate. It starts automatically after the local model is ready, shuts down when the keyboard leaves or the brief return handoff expires, and buffers audio only while you are actively dictating.
  • Allow Full Access (keyboard setting) — used for protected, device-local coordination between the keyboard and companion app and for receiving a temporary transcription result. The keyboard extension itself contains no networking, microphone, or speech-model code. On current iOS releases, Full Access can also affect whether a keyboard can open its companion app; if that launch is unavailable, you can open Open Blabber manually.

Bundled English model

Open Blabber supports English transcription only. The seven quantized Moonshine Tiny Streaming model assets—roughly 49 MB total—ship inside the app, so there is no first-use model download and transcription works offline immediately. The companion app owns the model and microphone; the memory-constrained keyboard extension contains neither. Moonshine Voice and the English model are MIT licensed, while Open Blabber is GPLv3.
